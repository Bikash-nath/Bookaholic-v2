Popular Answered Questions

How do you create a strong, unforgettable female lead?
Veronica Roth It sounds like a bit of a paradox, maybe, but you have to let your characters be weak and flawed in order to make them "strong"-- or complex and memor…moreIt sounds like a bit of a paradox, maybe, but you have to let your characters be weak and flawed in order to make them "strong"-- or complex and memorable, which I think is more important and probably just a different way of saying what you're asking. The flaws you give them can't just exist to make the character more colorful-- they have to create real, significant obstacles in your story. I think letting go of concerns about likability is important, too. Not that you can't have a likable character, but I think sometimes we misunderstand what makes characters lovable, which is when they feel like real people. And real people have weak moments, dark moments, sad moments. Real people are vulnerable and relatable.(less)

Do you have a writing routine?
Veronica Roth I do not! I get up and I try to write throughout the day. Sometimes it works and sometimes it doesn't. Sometimes I can feel that I just need a break f…moreI do not! I get up and I try to write throughout the day. Sometimes it works and sometimes it doesn't. Sometimes I can feel that I just need a break from even trying, and I let myself have that for weeks at a time, if my publication schedule permits it. I recognize that I am very fortunate to have that kind of flexibility, as most full time writers do not. But I'm really not someone who thrives with routines, and I never have been.(less)
